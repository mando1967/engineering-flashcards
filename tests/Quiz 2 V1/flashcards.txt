1. (3 points) The electric field at a distance r from a point charge Q is proportional to:
(a) Q/r
(b) Q/r²
(c) Q²/r
(d) Q/r³
(e) Q²/r²	Answer: (b) Q/r²
Explanation: Coulomb's law gives E = kQ/r². Electric field strength decreases with the square of distance from a point charge.

2. (3 points) Two point charges +Q and -Q are separated by a distance d. The electric field at the midpoint between them:
(a) is zero
(b) points toward +Q
(c) points toward -Q
(d) points perpendicular to the line joining them
(e) depends on the magnitude of Q	Answer: (c) points toward -Q
Explanation: Both charges create fields pointing toward -Q at the midpoint. The +Q charge creates a field pointing away from it (toward -Q), and the -Q charge creates a field pointing toward itself. These add up.

3. (3 points) A proton is placed in a uniform electric field. The proton will:
(a) remain at rest
(b) move in a circular path
(c) accelerate in the direction of the field
(d) accelerate opposite to the direction of the field
(e) oscillate about its initial position	Answer: (c) accelerate in the direction of the field
Explanation: Electric field direction is defined as the direction a positive charge would move. Since a proton is positive, it accelerates in the field direction with F = qE.

4. (3 points) The work done in moving a charge q through a potential difference V is:
(a) qV
(b) q/V
(c) V/q
(d) q²V
(e) V²/q	Answer: (a) qV
Explanation: Work = charge × potential difference. W = qV. This is the fundamental relationship between work, charge, and voltage. 1 Joule = 1 Coulomb × 1 Volt.

5. (3 points) A parallel-plate capacitor has capacitance C. If the plate separation is doubled while keeping everything else constant, the new capacitance is:
(a) 4C
(b) 2C
(c) C
(d) C/2
(e) C/4	Answer: (d) C/2
Explanation: Capacitance C = ε₀A/d. If d doubles, C is halved. Capacitance is inversely proportional to plate separation.

(a) 11Ω
(b) 6Ω
(c) 3Ω
(d) 1Ω

Explanation: For parallel resistors: 1/R_eq = 1/2 + 1/3 + 1/6 = 3/6 + 2/6 + 1/6 = 6/6 = 1, so R_eq = 1Ω.

7. (3 points) A wire of resistance R is stretched to twice its original length. Assuming the volume remains constant, the new resistance is:
(a) R/4
(b) R/2
(c) R
(d) 2R
(e) 4R	Answer: (e) 4R
Explanation: R = ρL/A. If length doubles and volume is constant, area halves. New R = ρ(2L)/(A/2) = 4ρL/A = 4R.

8. (3 points) In an RC circuit, the time constant τ represents:

10. (3 points) Kirchhoff's voltage law states that:
(a) the sum of currents entering a junction equals the sum leaving
(b) the sum of voltages around a closed loop is zero
(c) voltage equals current times resistance
(d) power equals voltage times current
(e) charge is conserved	Answer: (b) the sum of voltages around a closed loop is zero
Explanation: KVL is based on conservation of energy. Going around any closed loop, the algebraic sum of potential differences must equal zero.

20. (3 points) The equivalent resistance of resistors R₁ and R₂ in parallel is:
(a) R₁ + R₂
(b) R₁R₂
(c) R₁R₂/(R₁ + R₂)
(d) (R₁ + R₂)/R₁R₂
(e) R₁/R₂	Answer: (c) R₁R₂/(R₁ + R₂)
Explanation: From 1/R_eq = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂, we get R_eq = R₁R₂/(R₁ + R₂). This is the product-over-sum formula for two parallel resistors.
